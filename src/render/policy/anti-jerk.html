--- title: "Anti-Jerk Policy" layout: "default" ---

The core of our anti-jerk policy is pretty simple: don’t be a jerk.

Of course, if that were enough of a policy to weed jerks out of a community, it’d be a universal rule of the Internet by now. But there is an important component to take away here: if someone is being a jerk to you, we’ve got your back. If you see someone being a jerk to another member, we’ve got their back.

If, on the other hand, you insist on being a jerk to others then we reserve the right to fight you to the pain.

WebPlatform Specs is a harassment-free project. We will not tolerate harassment of any form, including related to gender, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, body size, race, or religion.

People are frequently wrong on the Internet. All the same, they are to be treated with respect. If you can’t find a constructive, helpful way in which to formulate your disagreement then most likely you don’t yourself have clear position to start with. Hint: if you find yourself constantly repeating the same point while others keep providing you with kind and helpful push-back, the odds are the jerk in the conversation is you. That’s okay, we’ve all been there. Just take a deep breath and move on.

Standards communities also have their own specific type of jerk that is worth mentioning here: the Process Jerk. A Process Jerk will find every manner and form of reason to block process by arguing process points, because they are seemingly unable to understand the fact that rules are made to fix problems, not to create them. Remember: be thoughtful.

Process Jerks will be dealt with with inane brutality.
